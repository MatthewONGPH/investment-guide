Why Isn't Keeping Money in the Bank Enough?

Bank savings are very safe — but the interest is only about 0.05% per year. The problem is, prices rise by about 2.5–3% every year.

📖 A Simple Everyday Example

Imagine $10 buys one bag of groceries today.
In 8 years, that same bag might cost $13.
But your $10 in the bank? It only grows to $10.04.

👉 The number barely moved — but you can no longer afford the same groceries.

The TDT Framework: T · D · T

Remember just three letters — TDT. Time, Diversify, Top Up. These are the three rules every successful investor follows:

T
⏳
Time — Stay the Course
Hold long-term and don't sell just because markets dip.
Like fine wine — the longer you wait, the better the result.
Fear and greed are the investor's worst enemies — stay calm.
D
🧺
Diversify — Spread the Risk
Invest across different types of assets and different countries.
Just like buying different groceries — never put all eggs in one basket.
If one area falls, the others still protect you.
T
🛒
Top Up — Buy on the Dip
When markets drop, it's like a supermarket sale — a great time to buy more at a cheaper price!
When others are scared and selling, we buy more — and profit when prices recover.
